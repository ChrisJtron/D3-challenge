Correlations Discovered Between Income And Obesity

Low income is at higher risk of obesity. There is an observable trend that as income goes up the obesity rate tends to fall. However, states such as Maryland, Alaska, and New Jersey have the highest income levels and still have medium to elevated obesity levels. In conclusion, very low income states have high obesity, but upper income states are not guaranteed low obesity rates.
